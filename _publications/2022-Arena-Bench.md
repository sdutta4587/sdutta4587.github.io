Strong ground motion parameters can exhibit different characteristics, with some focusing solely on motion amplitude, while others emphasize frequency content or time. Existing literature reviews indicate that the characterization of ground motion for a generalized case has not been fully addressed. In this study, ground motion parameters were derived using various datasets obtained from the PEER Center, employing a deterministic approach. Initially, an open-source software code called OpenSeismoMatlab was utilized to establish a reference for obtaining the parameters, addressing a primary concern. A total of 195 ground motions were analyzed, yielding parameters such as Basic Indices, Peak Indices, Cumulative Indices, Mixed Indices, as well as spectral parameters obtained through Fourier Spectra and Elastic Spectra. Correlation coefficient between these parameters were also calculated. The results provide insights into obtaining parameters directly for specific ground motions, offering a comprehensive understanding of their nature. This work concentrates on establishing a methodology for obtaining parameters applicable to generalized ground motion scenarios.
